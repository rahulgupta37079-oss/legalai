## üáÆüá≥ 1. Indian Constitutional Law

### Fundamental Rights (Part III, Articles 12-35)

#### Core Constitutional Concepts
- **Constitution of India**: Framework, features, structure (470 Articles, 25 Parts, 12 Schedules, 105 Amendments)
- **Fundamental Rights**: 6 categories of basic human rights guaranteed by Constitution

#### Right to Equality (Articles 14-18)
- **Article 14**: Equality before law and equal protection of laws
  - Negative and positive concepts
  - Reasonable classification doctrine
  - Landmark cases: Maneka Gandhi v Union, E.P. Royappa v State of TN
- **Article 15**: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth
  - Clauses for special provisions (women, children, backward classes, EWS)
  - 103rd Amendment: 10% EWS reservation
- **Article 16**: Equality of opportunity in public employment
  - Reservation provisions for SCs/STs
  - 77th & 81st Amendments on promotions and carry forward

#### Right to Freedom (Articles 19-22)
- **Article 19**: Protection of six freedoms (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession)
  - Available only to citizens
  - Reasonable restrictions framework
  - Shreya Singhal v Union case (Section 66A struck down)
- **Article 20**: Protection against conviction (ex post facto laws, double jeopardy, self-incrimination)
- **Article 21**: Protection of life and personal liberty
  - Most expansive fundamental right
  - Includes: dignity, livelihood, privacy, education, food, environment, speedy trial, legal aid, shelter, health
  - Procedure must be just, fair, and reasonable
  - Puttaswamy judgment 2017 (right to privacy)
- **Article 21A**: Right to free and compulsory education (6-14 years)
  - 86th Amendment, RTE Act 2009
  - 25% EWS reservation in private schools
- **Article 22**: Protection against arrest and detention
  - Rights of arrested persons and preventive detention provisions
  - 24-hour magistrate production requirement

#### Right Against Exploitation (Articles 23-24)
- **Article 23**: Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labour
  - Begar prohibition
  - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act 1956
- **Article 24**: Prohibition of child labour (below 14 years in hazardous employment)
  - Child Labour Act 1986 (amended 2016)
  - Complete prohibition below 14 years

#### Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25-28)
- **Article 25**: Freedom of conscience and religion
  - Profess, practice, propagate
  - Subject to public order, morality, health
  - Sikhs allowed to carry kirpan
- **Article 26**: Freedom to manage religious affairs
  - Establish institutions, manage affairs, own/acquire property
  - Essential religious practices doctrine

#### Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29-30)
- **Article 29**: Protection of interests of minorities
  - Conservation of language, script, culture
  - No discrimination in educational admission
- **Article 30**: Right of minorities to establish educational institutions
  - Religious and linguistic minorities
  - TMA Pai Foundation case clarification

#### Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)
- **Article 32**: "Heart and Soul of Constitution" (Dr. Ambedkar)
  - Right to move Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental rights
  - Five writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, Certiorari, Quo Warranto
  - Cannot be suspended except during emergency (Article 359)
  - Locus standi relaxed for PIL

### Directive Principles of State Policy (Part IV, Articles 36-51)
- Non-justiciable guidelines for governance
- Three categories:
  1. **Socialistic**: Articles 38, 39, 41, 42, 43, 47
  2. **Gandhian**: Articles 40, 43, 46, 47, 48
  3. **Liberal-intellectual**: Articles 44, 45, 48, 49, 50, 51
- Key principles: Social justice, adequate livelihood, equal pay, free legal aid, uniform civil code, education, environment protection, international peace

### Fundamental Duties (Part IV-A, Article 51A)
- Added by 42nd Amendment (1976)
- 11 duties (10 original + 1 by 86th Amendment 2002)
- Includes: Respect Constitution, cherish freedom ideals, uphold sovereignty, defend country, promote harmony, preserve culture, protect environment, develop scientific temper, safeguard public property, strive for excellence, educate children (6-14 years)

## üåç 2. International Human Rights Frameworks

### Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR, 1948)
- Adopted 10 December 1948 by UN General Assembly
- 30 Articles covering fundamental human rights
- Key rights: Life, liberty, security; freedom from slavery/torture; equality before law; effective remedy; fair trial; privacy; freedom of movement; asylum; nationality; marriage; property; freedom of thought/religion/expression; assembly; political participation; social security; work; rest; adequate living standard; education; cultural participation
- Non-binding but customary international law

### International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR, 1966)
- Entered into force 1976, 173 parties (India ratified 1979)
- 53 Articles in 6 Parts
- Enforces UDHR civil and political rights
- Key rights: Life; freedom from torture; prohibition of slavery; liberty and security; humane treatment of prisoners; freedom of movement; fair trial; recognition before law; privacy; freedom of thought/conscience/religion/expression; assembly; association; political participation; equality before law; minority rights
- Monitored by Human Rights Committee
- Optional Protocols: death penalty abolition, individual complaints

### International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR, 1966)
- Entered into force 1976, 171 parties (India ratified 1979)
- Economic, social, cultural rights treaty
- Key rights: Work; just work conditions; trade unions; social security; family protection; adequate standard of living; health; education; cultural life
- Progressive realization principle (implement gradually to maximum available resources)
- Monitored by Committee on ESC Rights
- Optional Protocol (2008): individual complaints

### Convention on Elimination of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW, 1979)
- International bill of rights for women
- 189 parties (India ratified 1993), 30 Articles
- Defines discrimination, state obligations, temporary special measures
- Coverage: Political/public life, education, employment, health, economic/social life, rural women, equality before law, marriage/family
- Monitored by CEDAW Committee
- Optional Protocol (2000): complaints procedure

### Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC, 1989)
- Most widely ratified human rights treaty
- 196 parties (India ratified 1992), 54 Articles
- Defines child as under 18 years
- Guiding principles: Non-discrimination; best interests of child; right to life/survival/development; respect for child's views
- Rights: Name/nationality; parental guidance; family reunification; protection from violence; health; education; rest/play/culture; protection from exploitation; juvenile justice
- Monitored by Committee on Rights of Child
- Optional Protocols: armed conflict, sale/prostitution/pornography, communications

### Convention Against Torture (CAT, 1984)
- Prohibits torture and cruel, inhuman, degrading treatment
- 173 parties (India not ratified, only signatory)
- Defines torture: severe pain/suffering intentionally inflicted by public official
- Obligations: Criminalize torture; jurisdiction; extradite or prosecute; no deportation to torture risk (non-refoulement); prevent torture; exclude torture evidence; victim redress
- Monitored by Committee Against Torture
- Optional Protocol (OPCAT): national prevention mechanisms

### Convention on Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD, 2006)
- First 21st century human rights treaty
- 191 parties (India ratified 2007), 50 Articles
- Purpose: Promote, protect, ensure equal rights for persons with disabilities
- Principles: Dignity, non-discrimination, participation, respect for difference, accessibility, equality, inclusion
- Rights: Equality before law; access to justice; liberty; freedom from exploitation; independent living; education; health; work; political participation; cultural life; accessibility
- Monitored by Committee on Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- India enacted Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016

## üè≠ 3. International Labour Standards (ILO Conventions)

### Overview
- International Labour Organization conventions since 1919
- 190 Conventions, 206 Recommendations
- India ratified 6 of 8 fundamental conventions

### 8 Fundamental Conventions (Core Labour Standards)

#### Freedom of Association
- **C87 (1948)**: Freedom of Association and Protection of Right to Organise (India not ratified)
- **C98 (1949)**: Right to Organise and Collective Bargaining

#### Forced Labour
- **C29 (1930)**: Forced Labour Convention
- **C105 (1957)**: Abolition of Forced Labour Convention

#### Child Labour
- **C138 (1973)**: Minimum Age Convention - minimum age 15 years (14 for developing countries) (India not ratified)
- **C182 (1999)**: Worst Forms of Child Labour Convention - prohibits slavery, trafficking, forced labour, prostitution, illicit activities, hazardous work for under 18

#### Equality
- **C100 (1951)**: Equal Remuneration Convention
- **C111 (1958)**: Discrimination (Employment and Occupation) Convention

### Other Important ILO Conventions
- **C81**: Labour Inspection
- **C129**: Labour Inspection in Agriculture
- **C144**: Tripartite Consultation

### ILO Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work (1998)
- Makes core standards binding even without ratification

### Child Labour in India
- **Constitutional**: Article 24 - no child under 14 in factories/mines/hazardous work
- **Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act 1986** (amended 2016):
  - Complete prohibition under 14 (except family business/entertainment with conditions)
  - 14-18 years: adolescent, no hazardous work
- **Penalties**: Imprisonment up to 2 years, fines
- **Implementation challenges**: Informal sector, poverty, enforcement gaps

### Forced Labour
- **ILO Definition**: Work or service extracted under menace of penalty, not voluntarily offered
- **Exceptions**: Military service, civic obligations, prison labour (not hired to private), emergencies, minor communal services
- **Modern forms**: Human trafficking, bonded labour, debt bondage, forced domestic work
- **India**: Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act 1976 - abolished bonded labour, provides rehabilitation; penalties up to 3 years imprisonment
- **Global estimate**: 25 million in forced labour worldwide (ILO 2021)

## üå± 4. Environmental Law

### Indian Environmental Statutes
- **Constitutional Provisions**:
  - Article 48A (DPSP): Protect environment, forests, wildlife
  - Article 51A(g) (Fundamental Duty): Protect environment
- **Environment (Protection) Act 1986**: Umbrella legislation post-Bhopal disaster
- **Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974**
- **Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981**
- **Forest (Conservation) Act 1980**
- **Wildlife Protection Act 1972**
- **Biological Diversity Act 2002**
- **National Green Tribunal Act 2010**: Established NGT (specialized environmental court)

### Environmental Law Principles
1. **Precautionary Principle**: Prevent harm even without scientific certainty
2. **Polluter Pays**: Polluter bears cost of pollution control and compensation
3. **Sustainable Development**: Balance present needs with future generations
4. **Public Trust Doctrine**: Natural resources held in trust for public
5. **Intergenerational Equity**: Protect resources for future generations

### Climate Change Law

#### International Framework
- **Stockholm Declaration 1972**: First global environmental conference
- **Rio Declaration 1992**: Sustainable development principles
- **UNFCCC (1992)**: Framework convention on climate change
- **Kyoto Protocol (1997)**: Binding emission targets for developed countries
- **Paris Agreement (2015)**:
  - 197 parties
  - Limit warming to well below 2¬∞C, pursue 1.5¬∞C
  - Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs)
  - Climate finance mechanisms

#### India's Climate Commitments
- **Ratified Paris Agreement**: 2016
- **NDC Commitments**:
  - Reduce emissions intensity of GDP by 45% by 2030 (from 2005 levels)
  - Achieve 50% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil sources by 2030
  - Create carbon sink of 2.5-3 billion tonnes CO2 equivalent through forests
- **National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)**: 8 missions
- **Energy Conservation Act 2001** (amended 2022): Added carbon markets, green hydrogen
- **Climate Change Act**: Under consideration

## üí° 5. Intellectual Property Rights

### Overview
- India is party to WTO TRIPS Agreement (1995)
- Institutional framework: Controller General of Patents, Designs and Trademarks; Copyright Office
- IPAB abolished 2021, functions transferred to High Courts

### Patents Act, 1970 (amended 2005)
- **Duration**: 20 years from filing
- **Patentability criteria**: Novel, non-obvious (inventive step), industrial applicability
- **Exclusions (Section 3)**:
  - Abstract ideas, mathematical methods, business methods
  - Computer programs per se
  - Plant/animal varieties (except microorganisms)
  - Methods of agriculture/horticulture
  - Treatment of humans/animals
  - Traditional knowledge
- **Section 3(d)**: Incremental pharmaceutical innovations (efficacy threshold)
  - Novartis Glivec case 2013: Rejected patent for minor modification
- **Compulsory Licensing (Section 84)**:
  - If patent not worked in India
  - Reasonable requirements not met
  - Not available at affordable price
  - First compulsory license: Natco v Bayer (Sorafenib, 2012)
- **Patent Prosecution**: Application ‚Üí Examination (18 months) ‚Üí Opposition (pre/post grant) ‚Üí Grant ‚Üí Maintenance fees

### Copyright Act, 1957 (amended 2012)
- **Protected Works**: Literary, dramatic, musical, artistic, cinematograph films, sound recordings
- **Duration**:
  - Author's life + 60 years (literary/dramatic/musical/artistic)
  - 60 years from publication (films, sound recordings, photographs, posthumous, government/PSU works)
- **Rights**:
  - **Economic**: Reproduction, adaptation, public performance, communication to public
  - **Moral**: Paternity, integrity (inalienable)
- **Fair Dealing Exceptions (Section 52)**: Research, criticism, review, news reporting, education
- **Performers' Rights**: Chapter VIII
- **Digital Rights**: 2012 amendments address internet, statutory licenses, copyright societies regulation
- **Infringement**:
  - **Civil**: Damages, injunction, accounts
  - **Criminal**: Imprisonment up to 3 years, fines up to ‚Çπ2 lakh
- **Copyright Board**: Adjudicates disputes

### Trademarks Act, 1999
- **Protection**: Marks (word, device, logo, color, sound, smell, shape) distinguishing goods/services
- **Registration**: 10 years, renewable indefinitely
- **Types**: Individual marks, collective marks, certification marks
- **Classification**: 45 classes (Nice Classification)
- **Examination**:
  - **Absolute grounds (Section 9)**: Descriptive, generic, deceptive, prohibited
  - **Relative grounds (Section 11)**: Similarity, prior rights
- **Well-known Marks**: Enhanced protection beyond registered classes (Section 11(6)-(9))
- **Infringement (Section 29)**:
  - Identical/similar mark
  - Identical/similar goods/services
  - Likelihood of confusion
  - Dilution of well-known mark
- **Passing Off**: Common law remedy even without registration
- **Domain Names**: INDRP (.in domains), UDRP (international)
- **Offenses**: False trademark, false trade description
  - Imprisonment up to 3 years, fines up to ‚Çπ2 lakh

## üè¢ 6. Commercial & Corporate Law

### Companies Act, 2013
- Comprehensive corporate law replacing 1956 Act
- **Structure**: 470 Sections, 7 Schedules
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Company formation (Sections 3-22)
  - Share capital and debentures (Sections 43-71)
  - Prospectus (Sections 23-35)
  - Acceptance of deposits (Section 73-76)
  - Management and administration (Sections 149-172)
  - Meetings (Sections 96-122)
  - Accounts and audit (Sections 128-148)
  - Insider trading prohibition (Section 195)
  - Fraud (Section 447): Imprisonment up to 10 years, fine up to ‚Çπ50 crore
  - Oppression and mismanagement (Sections 241-246)
  - National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT - Sections 408-434)
- **Corporate Social Responsibility (Section 135)**:
  - Companies meeting criteria must spend 2% of average net profit on CSR
- **Key Regulators**: Registrar of Companies (RoC), Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)
- **Integration**: Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016

### Competition Act, 2002
- Prevents anti-competitive practices, promotes competition
- **Competition Commission of India (CCI)**: Regulatory authority
- **Prohibited Practices**:
  1. **Anti-competitive Agreements (Section 3)**:
     - **Horizontal**: Cartels (price fixing, bid rigging, market sharing)
     - **Vertical**: Tie-in, exclusive distribution, refusal to deal
     - Test: Appreciable Adverse Effect on Competition (AAEC)
  2. **Abuse of Dominant Position (Section 4)**:
     - Unfair/discriminatory pricing
     - Predatory pricing
     - Denial of market access
     - Tying/bundling
  3. **Combinations (Section 5-6)**:
     - Mergers/acquisitions above thresholds require CCI approval
- **Penalties**: Up to 10% of average turnover for 3 preceding years
- **Leniency Program**: For cartel whistleblowers
- **Appeals**: Competition Appellate Tribunal (now NCLAT)
- **Director General**: Investigates upon CCI direction
- **Bid-Rigging**: Section 3(3)(d) prohibition
- **Settlement/Commitment**: Procedure available

### Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 (IBC)
- Consolidated insolvency law replacing multiple legislations
- **Time-bound Process**: 180 days (extendable to 330 days)
- **Applicability**: Companies, LLPs, Partnerships, Individuals (personal insolvency notified partially)
- **Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP - Sections 6-32)**:
  1. **Initiation**: Financial Creditor/Operational Creditor/Corporate Debtor
  2. **Admission**: NCLT admits if default (minimum ‚Çπ1 crore)
  3. **Resolution Professional (RP)**: Takes management
  4. **Committee of Creditors (CoC)**: Approves resolution plan by 66% vote
  5. **Outcome**: Plan approved ‚Üí NCLT sanctions; else liquidation
- **Key Features**:
  - Moratorium on recovery during CIRP
  - Priority: Secured creditors > Workmen dues (24 months) > Unsecured creditors > Shareholders
- **SARFAESI Act 2002**: Co-exists for secured creditors' individual action
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI)**: Regulates IPs, agencies

## üåê 7. International Trade & Investment

### World Trade Organization (WTO)
- **Established**: 1995, successor to GATT 1947
- **Members**: 164 (India founding member)
- **Key Agreements**:
  1. **GATT 1994**: Trade in goods
     - MFN (Most Favored Nation)
     - National treatment
     - Tariff binding
  2. **GATS**: Trade in services
     - 4 modes of supply
  3. **TRIPS**: Intellectual property minimum standards
  4. **SPS**: Sanitary and phytosanitary measures
  5. **TBT**: Technical barriers to trade
  6. **Agriculture Agreement**: Domestic support, export subsidies, market access
  7. **Dispute Settlement Understanding (DSU)**: Binding WTO dispute resolution
- **Principles**:
  - Non-discrimination (MFN - Art I GATT; National treatment - Art III GATT)
  - Tariff binding
  - Transparency
  - Special and differential treatment for developing countries
- **India's Position**: Doha Round deadlock, focus on bilateral/regional FTAs, RCEP negotiation withdrawn 2019

### Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA)
- Liberalized regime replacing FERA 1973
- **Objective**: Facilitate external trade and payments, promote orderly forex market
- **Regulates**:
  - **Current Account Transactions (Section 5)**: Generally permitted
  - **Capital Account Transactions (Section 6)**: Requires RBI approval for specified transactions
  - **Export/Import of Currency (Section 7)**
  - **Repatriation Requirement (Section 4)**
- **FDI Policy**: Automatic route (no approval) and government route (approval required)
  - Sectoral caps and conditions
- **FII/FPI Investments**: Foreign Institutional/Portfolio Investors
- **External Commercial Borrowings (ECB)**: Foreign currency loans
- **Overseas Direct Investment (ODI)**: Indian companies investing abroad
- **Liberalized Remittance Scheme (LRS)**: $250,000 per person per year
- **Enforcement Directorate (ED)**: Enforces FEMA
- **Penalties**: Up to 3 times sum involved
- **FEMA (Non-Debt Instruments) Rules 2019**: Consolidated FDI rules
- **Reserve Bank**: Regulates through Master Directions

## üí∞ 8. Taxation Law

### Goods and Services Tax (GST)
- **Introduced**: 1 July 2017 through 101st Constitutional Amendment
- **Nature**: Destination-based, multi-stage, value-added tax on supply of goods and services
- **Structure**:
  1. **CGST**: Central GST (intra-state supply)
  2. **SGST**: State GST (intra-state)
  3. **IGST**: Integrated GST (inter-state, imports)
  4. **UTGST**: Union Territory GST
- **Rate Structure**: Four-tier
  - 0% (essential goods)
  - 5%
  - 12%
  - 18%
  - 28% (luxury/sin goods)
- **Composition Scheme**: For small taxpayers (turnover up to ‚Çπ1.5 crore)
- **Input Tax Credit (ITC)**: Mechanism to avoid cascading tax
- **GST Council (Article 279A)**:
  - Constitutional body
  - Recommends rates, exemptions
  - Decision: 2/3rd states, 1/3rd Centre
- **GST Network (GSTN)**: IT backbone
- **Compliance**:
  - Monthly returns: GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-3B (summary)
  - Annual return: GSTR-9
- **E-way Bill**: For goods movement above threshold
- **Anti-profiteering Provisions**: Ensure ITC benefit passed to consumers
- **Tribunals**: Pending establishment

### Income Tax Act, 1961
- **Direct tax on income**
- **Administered by**: Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT)
- **Assessee Categories**: Individuals, HUF, Firms, Companies, AOPs, BOIs
- **Income Heads**:
  1. Salaries
  2. House property
  3. Business/Profession
  4. Capital gains
  5. Other sources
- **Residential Status**: Determines tax liability
  - Resident
  - Non-resident
  - Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR)
- **Tax Regimes**:
  - **Old Regime**: Higher rates with deductions/exemptions
  - **New Regime** (optional): Lower rates without deductions/exemptions
- **TDS/TCS**: Tax Deducted/Collected at Source
- **Advance Tax**: Pay-as-you-earn principle
- **Return Filing**: ITR-1 to ITR-7 (different forms for different assessees)
- **Assessment Types**:
  - Self-assessment
  - Summary assessment
  - Scrutiny assessment
  - Best judgment assessment
- **Appeals Hierarchy**: CIT(A) ‚Üí ITAT ‚Üí High Court ‚Üí Supreme Court
- **Penalties**:
  - Under-reporting: 50% of tax
  - Misreporting: 200% of tax
  - Failure to file return
  - Concealment
- **Search and Seizure**: Section 132 powers
- **Transfer Pricing**: Sections 92-92F (for international transactions)
- **GAAR**: General Anti-Avoidance Rules (to check tax avoidance)

- **Procedural law** governing civil litigation in India

    - Bailable offenses

  - **FIR (Section 154)**: First Information Report
  - **Investigation (Sections 154-176)**: Police powers, procedure
  - **Trial Procedure (Chapters XVIII-XXI)**:
    - Warrant cases
    - Summons cases
    - Sessions trials
  - **Appeals and Revisions (Chapters XXIX-XXX)**
  - **Maintenance (Chapter IX, Section 125)**: Spousal and child support
  - **Compounding of Offenses (Section 320)**: Settlement
- **Important Sections**:
  - **Section 161**: Examination by police
  - **Section 164**: Statement before magistrate
  - **Section 313**: Accused examination
  - **Section 482**: High Court inherent powers
- **Upcoming**: BNSS 2023 to replace CrPC from July 2024

### Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)
- **Substantive criminal law** of India
- **Structure**: 511 Sections in 23 Chapters
- **Key Chapters**:
  - **General Exceptions (Chapter IV, Sec 76-106)**: Defenses
  - **Offenses Against State (Chapter VI)**: Sedition, waging war
  - **Offenses Against Public Tranquility (Chapter VIII)**: Rioting, unlawful assembly
  - **Offenses Against Human Body (Chapter XVI, Sec 299-377)**:
    - Murder (Section 302)
    - Culpable homicide (Sections 299-304)
    - Hurt (Sections 319-338)
    - Rape (Sections 375-376)
    - Kidnapping (Sections 359-369)
  - **Offenses Against Property (Chapter XVII, Sec 378-462)**:
    - Theft (Sections 378-382)
    - Robbery/Dacoity (Sections 390-402)
    - Criminal breach of trust (Sections 405-409)
    - Cheating (Sections 415-420)
  - **Offenses Relating to Documents (Chapter XVIII)**: Forgery
- **Important Sections**:
  - **Section 302**: Murder (punishment: death or life imprisonment)
  - **Section 307**: Attempt to murder
  - **Section 375-376**: Rape and sexual assault
  - **Section 420**: Cheating
  - **Section 498A**: Cruelty by husband or relatives
- **Maximum Punishment**: Death penalty or life imprisonment
- **Upcoming**: BNS 2023 to replace IPC from July 2024

### Indian Evidence Act, 1872 (IEA)
- **Law of evidence** for all judicial proceedings
- **Structure**: 167 Sections in 3 Parts
- **Part I: Relevancy of Facts (Sec 5-55)**: When facts are relevant
- **Part II: On Proof (Sec 56-100)**:
  - Oral evidence
  - Documentary evidence
  - Burden of proof
  - Presumptions
- **Part III: Production and Effect of Evidence (Sec 101-167)**:
  - Examination of witnesses
  - Expert evidence
  - Character evidence
  - Dying declaration
- **Key Concepts**:
  - **Facts in Issue**: Main facts to be proved
  - **Relevant Facts**: Connected to facts in issue
  - **Admissions (Sections 17-23)**: Voluntary acknowledgment
  - **Confessions (Sections 24-30)**: Admission of guilt
  - **Dying Declaration (Section 32)**: Statement by person about to die
  - **Expert Opinion (Sections 45-51)**: Specialist testimony
  - **Hearsay**: Generally inadmissible
  - **Best Evidence Rule (Section 91)**: Original document required
  - **Burden of Proof (Sections 101-114)**: Who must prove
- **Important Section**: Section 65B (electronic evidence certificate)
- **Upcoming**: BSA 2023 to replace IEA from July 2024

## üìã 10. Major Regulatory Acts

### Right to Information Act, 2005 (RTI Act)
- **Purpose**: Empower citizens to access information from public authorities
- **Objective**: Promote transparency and accountability
- **Key Features**:
  - **Right to Information (Section 3)**: Every citizen has right
  - **Public Authorities (Section 5)**: Must appoint Public Information Officers (PIOs)
  - **Time Limit (Section 7)**: Information provided within 30 days
  - **First Appeal (Section 19)**: To senior officer
  - **Second Appeal (Section 19)**: To Information Commission
- **Exemptions (Section 8)**:
  - Security, foreign relations
  - Cabinet papers
  - Trade secrets
  - Parliamentary privilege
  - Personal information
  - Commercial confidence
- **RTI Overrides**: Official Secrets Act 1923
- **Information Commissions**:
  - **Central (CIC)**: For central government
  - **State (SIC)**: For state governments
- **Penalties (Section 20)**: For non-compliance by PIOs
- **Landmark Cases**: Namit Sharma v Union (2013)

### Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- **Replaced**: Consumer Protection Act 1986
- **Purpose**: Protect consumer rights against unfair trade practices
- **Key Definitions**:
  - Consumer
  - Defect
  - Unfair trade practice
  - E-commerce
- **Consumer Rights (Section 2(9))**: 6 fundamental rights
- **Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions**:
  1. **District Commission (Section 28)**: Up to ‚Çπ1 crore

- **Key Provisions**:
  - **Product Liability (Sections 82-87)**
  - **Unfair Contracts (Section 16)**

  - **Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA - Sections 10-32)**: Regulatory body
- **E-commerce Provisions (Section 2(16), Rules 2020)**
- **Mediation**: ADR mechanism
- **Appeal Hierarchy**: District ‚Üí State ‚Üí National Commission ‚Üí Supreme Court
- **Simplified Procedures**: Fast disposal

### Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
- **Based on**: UNCITRAL Model Law
- **Purpose**: Govern domestic and international commercial arbitration
- **Key Provisions**:
  - **Arbitration Agreement (Section 7)**: Written agreement to arbitrate
  - **Appointment of Arbitrators (Section 11)**: Procedure
  - **Jurisdiction (Section 16)**: Kompetenz-kompetenz (arbitrator decides jurisdiction)
  - **Interim Measures (Section 9)**: Court can grant before arbitral tribunal constituted
  - **Award (Sections 31-32)**: Final decision
  - **Setting Aside (Section 34)**: Challenge in court (limited grounds)
  - **Enforcement (Section 36)**: Award as decree
- **Amendments**:
  - **2015 Amendment**:
    - Time limit: 12 months for award
    - Reduced court intervention
    - Fast track: 6 months
  - **2019 Amendment**:
    - Arbitration Council of India
    - Automatic stay on award removed
    - Confidentiality provisions
  - **2021 Amendment**:
    - Unconditional stay on awards in case of fraud or corruption
- **International Commercial Arbitration**: Part II
- **Conciliation**: Part III

## üîê 11. Cybercrime & Data Protection

### Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act)
- **India's primary legislation** for cybercrimes and electronic commerce
- **Amended**: 2008 (significant strengthening)
- **Key Provisions**:
  - **Section 43**: Unauthorized access, data theft (compensation)
  - **Section 66**: Computer-related offenses (imprisonment up to 3 years)
  - **Section 66A**: Offensive messages (struck down by Supreme Court 2015 in Shreya Singhal case)
  - **Section 66B**: Receiving stolen computer resources
  - **Section 66C**: Identity theft
  - **Section 66D**: Cheating by impersonation
  - **Section 66E**: Privacy violation
  - **Section 66F**: Cyber terrorism (life imprisonment)
  - **Section 67**: Publishing obscene material
  - **Section 70**: Protected systems (critical infrastructure)
- **2008 Amendments**:
  - Added identity theft, cyber terrorism
  - Intermediate liability (Section 79)
  - Enhanced penalties
- **Institutional Framework**:
  - Cyber Appellate Tribunal
  - Adjudicating officers
- **Criticism**:
  - Low conviction rates
  - Inadequate investigation capacity
  - Lack of comprehensive data protection (addressed by DPDP Act 2023)

### Budapest Convention on Cybercrime (2001)
- **First comprehensive international treaty** on cybercrime
- **Opened by**: Council of Europe
- **Ratifications**: 68+ countries worldwide
- **India**: Not ratified (only observer)
- **Coverage**:
  - Illegal access
  - Illegal interception
  - Data interference
  - System interference
  - Computer-related fraud
  - Child pornography
  - Copyright infringement
- **Requirements**:
  - Harmonize domestic laws
  - Improve investigative techniques
  - 24/7 contact points
  - International cooperation
  - Mutual legal assistance
- **India's Position**: Concerns over sovereignty, data localization, human rights

### UN Cybercrime Convention (2024)
- **Newly adopted** international treaty (2024)
- **Focus**: International cooperation, capacity building
- **Key Elements**:
  - Electronic evidence gathering
  - Extradition for cyber offenses
  - Technical assistance
  - Prevention measures
  - Human rights protections
- **Difference from Budapest**: Emphasizes cooperation over substantive crimes
- **Status**: Countries in process of ratification as of 2024

### Data Protection

#### Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)
- **India's comprehensive data protection regime**
- **Key Features**:
  - Consent requirements
  - Data principal rights (access, correction, erasure, grievance)
  - Data fiduciary obligations
  - Cross-border transfer rules
  - Children's data protection
  - Data Protection Board (regulatory authority)
- **Penalties**: For non-compliance
- **Exemptions**: Government processing for specified purposes

#### IT Act Section 43A
- **Compensation** for negligent data handling
- Body corporate must protect sensitive personal data
- Reasonable security practices and procedures

#### IT Rules 2011
- **Security practices** for body corporates
- Privacy policy requirements
- Incident response

#### GDPR Compliance
- **EU GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) - gold standard
- Impacts Indian companies serving EU citizens
- Requirements: Consent, data subject rights, data protection officer, breach notification
